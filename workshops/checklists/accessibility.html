Notes on accessibility for workshop hosts, instructors, and helpers.

Before the Workshop

Preamble: can make life easier for everyone if event planners consider things in advance rather than forcing people to ask for things. Things to think about:

  • Does the sign-up form ask if learners need arrangements made ahead of time (e.g. sign-language interpreters, large-print printouts, special dietary needs)?
  • Is the room easily accessible to those who cannot use stairs? Are the elevators available at all times? Are there accesssible washrooms nearby?
  • Would there be any problem with having a service animal in the room? [Shouldn't be, unless it's a wet lab.]
  • Is the room lighting sufficiently bright to allow lip-reading? Is the projector screen large enough to be easily read?
  • If the room is large or noisy, is a microphone available for the instructors?
  • WHAT ELSE?

If learners indicate that advance arrangements are needed, the host needs to follow-up on this to make sure things are ready, and let the instructors know if action is required on their part.
