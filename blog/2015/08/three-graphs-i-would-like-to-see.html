The first tells us how much political interference skews computing priorities. (Off the record, everyone will admit that bureaucrats' desire for photo ops with big iron takes precedence over mere research.) The second will show the mis-match between where computing dollars and euros are spent, and what most scientists actually do, while the third will show that training doesn't just help scientists do more research: it also improves the return on investment in highly-skilled support staff, who would much rather be helping people parallelize their code than resurrect files from former grad students' accounts.
