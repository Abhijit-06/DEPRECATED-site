I started out as molecular biologist postdoc working in functional genomics. My boss at the time said "You! Learn to program". So I put down my pipettor and learned how to code. An autodidact, I committing just about every imaginable sin against best practices. I eventually started to interact with others of my kind, repented, developed an interest in outreach and training and became an evangelist bent on helping others see light and avoid the mistakes I made as a neophyte bioinformatician. The past ten years I contributed to various scientific computing training efforts with the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory "Programming for Biology" course, the Generic Model Organism Database Project, the iPlant Collaborative and, most recently, Software Carpentry.
